About Me

Elementary bilingual teacher with minor in disability and inclusion, Universidad de los Andes. I have done internships in 2nd, 4th and 5th grade in various private bilingual schools in Santiago, Chile, leading classes of between 27-40 students. I have knowledge of psychological first aid, sign language, CLIL and writers and reader’s workshops. I stand out for having leadership and teamwork skills, as well as the ability to connect with my students. I am interested in continuing to develop in the area of ​​NEE, TICS and Engagement. I have an advanced level of English. In this webpage you will be able to find all what I have done during the five years I studied for being able to become a teacher. So I invite you to get to know me better and have fun!

Deepen Practices

Lengua de Señas

Nivel: A1

Basic course of Chilean sign language, in which I learned how to sign greetings, feelings, emotions, numbers, months, days, actions, family members and time.

Primeros Auxilios Psicológicos

Course focused on how to face and manage panic attacks or some uncontrolled psychological reactions with a person who is with you.

Liderazgo Público

Course aimed at young university students who have a vocation for public service, and who want to promote the ideas of freedom and justice from different areas of social life. They talked about education, economy, history, poverty, freedom, among others.

Liderazgo Social

Course based on the characteristics of a good leaders and how a good leader leads their team, how to start new projects and how to deal with failure.

Positive Education for Young Learners

Estrategias de Engagement

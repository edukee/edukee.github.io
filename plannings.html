Colonial Heritage

This lesson is centered on the students because students are responsible of their learning, they must be able to be experts about their colonial heritage/patrimony, and then teach the rest of the class about that corresponding patrimony. If students are not able to organize themselves as a group and reflect, they will not be able to achieve the goal of the class.

Problem Solving

This class in centered on word problems of the four operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division). The goal of this class is to practice and remember the steps students need to follow to be able to answer a word problem successfully. This class is more teacher centered because I think that sometimes students also need explanations from the teacher and not always be students the ones that solve how to do it, since in this case not understanding word problems would make students develop anxiety and stress.

Gas Exchange

This class is a very concrete class where students must inquire and then make a real model about the gas exchange. I think that, especially in Natural Science, we need more of these types of activities because they make students construct their learning based on a fun activity and a concrete way in which they can represent what they are learning. This way the learning is more meaningful for them.

Exam Social Science on Educación Cívica

This is a planning for a whole unit of civic education in Chile. The unit consists in 6 inquiry classes where students will try to answer the question: ¿Cómo se caracteriza al Chile democrático? By doing different types of activities, including inquiry, model, charts, reading, creating an essay, among others. The unit has different types of assessments, formative and summative. These are critical for informing the teacher and students of their progress class by class. First, you will find a summary of the whole unit and then you are going to find the details of three of the lessons with their corresponding materials.

Comprensión Lectora

This class is a more traditional way of teaching where the teacher teaches a content and then students must complete a worksheet. However, you will find two aspect that will make this class much more meaningful for students. First, before the teacher teaches what is explicit and implicit, she shows real examples and make students reflect and get to a real definition and example of explicit and implicit types of questions. Second, the worksheet has a text and questions that are more challenging, so students can see that they can achieve challenging things and that they are not always going to do fun activities to learn.
